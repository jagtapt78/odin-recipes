Kheema Recipe

Kheema is a traditional curry dish from many countries in South Asia.

It consists of ground meat, onions, tomatoes, some herbs and spices, and water for the curry. In my family, for a lighter taste, we grew up eating this curry with ground turkey, usually 93% lean. Feel free to use whatever ground meat you like -- it tastes great with beef, lamb, goat, chicken, or a blend.

Curry itself is a broad term for many kinds of foods. Most curries incorporate sauteed onions and tomatoes in a two-stage process to create a delicious flavor base to roast your spices in.

After you make this spiced mix, you add your ground meat and cook. My family usually pressure cooks the curry to make it quickly without compromising on flavor. This also makes the meat a little more tender, which is good if you're using a meat like beef or lamb. However, it's optional.

Ingredients:

  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 neutral cooking oil
  • 2 diced tomatoes OR 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 lb ground turkey (or other ground meat)
  • 2 tbsp each of:
    • garam masala
    • chili powder
    • cumin
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 6-12 whole cloves
  • 1/3 stick of cinnamon, broken into pieces
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • salt to taste
  • optional: frozen green beans or spinach for nutrition!
  • 1/3 minced cilantro (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Prep all your vegetables ahead of time.
  2. If eating with rice, start your rice cooker now.
  3. Heat oil on medium high heat. When hot, add onion.
  4. Cook onion till browned, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add your dry spices.
  6. Once spices are lightly toasted, add your tomatoes and salt.
  7. Once oil starts to shimmer on the surface of the tomato and onion mixture, add your ground meat and frozen vegetables.
  8. Cook for 30-40 minutes. If using instant pot, put it on the high pressure for 25 min.
  9. Add cilantro for garnish and serve.
Optional: serve with a slice of lime and chopped red onion on top of toasted buns. Or eat mixed in with plain rice.
